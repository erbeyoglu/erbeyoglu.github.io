Paid or written off? Slides 55–61

An account ages month by month: from each age it is either paid, or slides one month closer to being written off as bad debt after month 3. Send 1000 new accounts through and compare with the absorption probabilities.

collected: — bad debt: —

An absorbing chain (slide 52): Paid and Bad absorb; the age states are transient.
spoiler — open after the class has answeredSolving the f-equations (slide 60) gives P(new account → bad) = 0.6 × 0.5 × 0.4 × 0.3 = 0.036. With $100 000 of monthly sales, about 12 × $100 000 × 3.6% = $43 200 per year goes uncollected (slide 61).

Design Freezco’s warranty Slides 62–66

Fridges fail with 3% in year 1, 5% in year 2, 7% in year 3. Freezco replaces any failure inside the warranty for $500 and sells 10 000 fridges a year. Choose the warranty length and read the bill.

The chain ages a fridge year by year with absorbing “replaced” exits (slide 63).
spoiler — open after the class has answeredFraction replaced under 3 years: 0.03 + 0.97·0.05 + 0.97·0.95·0.07 ≈ 0.1430; under 2 years: 0.0785. Cutting the warranty to 2 years saves 10 000 × (0.1430 − 0.0785) × $500 = $322 500 (slide 66) — before angry customers are priced in.

How long until HTH? Slide 67

Flip a fair coin until the pattern H T H appears. Class vote first: is the expected number of tosses 6, 8, or 10? Then flip — once, and a thousand times.

spoiler — open after the class has answeredTrack the useful prefix as a Markov chain: ∅ → H → HT → HTH. Mean first passage equations (slide 43): μ∅ = 1 + ½μ∅ + ½μ_H, μ_H = 1 + ½μ_H + ½μ_HT, μ_HT = 1 + ½μ∅. Solving: μ∅ = 10. (The sneaky part: from HT, a tail throws you ALL the way back to ∅.)
