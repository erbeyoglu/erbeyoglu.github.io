Gradient search: climb the hill Slides 13–29

The arrow is the gradient ∇f — the steepest-ascent direction, always perpendicular to the contour. Each step moves along the gradient until f stops increasing (the optimal step size t*), then re-aims. Click anywhere to choose a starting point, then step. Why doesn’t it go straight to the top?

iterx′∇f(x′)t*f(x′)

The procedure (slide 24): express f(x′ + t∇f(x′)) as a function of t, maximize over t ≥ 0, move, repeat until ‖∇f‖ ≤ ε. Consecutive directions are orthogonal — that is the zigzag. For minimization, walk against the gradient instead (slide 29).
