The time in a state must be exponential — it is the ONLY memoryless continuous distribution (slides 8–10), and memorylessness is exactly the Markov property in continuous time. E[T_up] = 1/q, E[T_down] = 1/μ, so the long-run share of up-time is \(\frac{1/q}{1/q + 1/\mu} = \frac{\mu}{q+\mu}\). It is the same memorylessness you tested on Week 10’s 10-year-old radio.
